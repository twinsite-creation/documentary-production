01 Shadows of Kyiv 2022
Feature 94 min Ukraine · Poland

Shadows
of Kyiv

2022

A city remembers everything.
Its people are learning to forget.

Over eighteen months, Marta Hryhorenko followed four residents of Kyiv's historic districts — a retired archivist, a young musician, a former Soviet engineer, and a teenage street artist — each living in a different relationship with the city's layered past. Shot in the weeks before and after a period of significant political upheaval, the film becomes a document of a city in the last moment before it changed irrevocably. The camera found not nostalgia — but something rawer.

The Last

Three voices. One century.
What happens when they go silent?

03 Dust & Memory 2020
Feature 112 min UA · DE · GE

Dust &
Memory

2020

Five cities. Two years.
What do we choose to remember?

Shot across Kyiv, Lviv, Warsaw, Tbilisi, and Leipzig over two years, this film follows the traces that ordinary people leave in the fabric of cities: photographs pinned to fences, inscriptions worn into doorframes, the routes old people still walk to houses that no longer exist. A meditation on urban memory and architectural grief. Co-produced with ZDF/Arte.

05 Silent Archive 2019
Short 24 min Ukraine

Silent
Archive

2019

The films they didn't want you to see.
The cinema they almost erased.

Silent Archive journeys through the forgotten footage of Ukrainian cinema from the 1960s — films shelved, cut, or quietly destroyed by Soviet censors — and asks what these fragments reveal about a suppressed artistic identity. Working with film historians, archivists, and surviving members of the Ukrainian Poetic Cinema movement, the film reconstructs not what was lost, but why. The gaps in the archive are as telling as what survives.

The People
Behind the Frame

A tight-knit collective of filmmakers, editors, and producers united by a single conviction: that the most important stories are the ones that haven't been told yet.

01 / 03

Marta Hryhorenko

Director
Award-winning documentary director. Films that ask difficult questions of history — and refuse easy answers.

Marta Hryhorenko is an award-winning documentary director whose work spans a decade of filmmaking in Eastern Europe. Her films have screened at IDFA, Berlinale, Venice, and Sundance — and have been broadcast in over forty countries.

She studied directing at the Kyiv National I.K. Karpenko-Kary Theatre, Cinema and Television University and later completed a fellowship at the Berlinale Talents programme. Her approach is rooted in what she calls "patient cinema" — long-form observation that refuses to arrive at conclusions before the subject is ready.

Marta currently divides her time between Kyiv and Warsaw, where she is in development on a new feature examining the post-war reconstruction of identity in southern Ukraine.

02 / 03

Dmytro Savchenko

Producer
Fourteen features, twenty shorts. Radical trust in the director's instinct and the audience's intelligence.

Dmytro Savchenko has produced fourteen documentary features and over twenty shorts across Europe and the Middle East. He entered the industry as a fixer in conflict zones and carries that discipline into every production — calm under pressure, precise in everything.

His producing philosophy is one of radical trust: in the director's instinct, in the subject's time, and in the audience's intelligence. He does not rush stories to their conclusions.

Dmytro holds an MA in International Film Business from the London Film School and serves on the advisory board of DocuDays UA International Human Rights Documentary Film Festival.

03 / 03

Olena Bondar

Editor
Assembles silence as much as image. Her editing shapes the rhythm that makes each film breathe.

Olena Bondar has spent fifteen years in the cutting room, shaping documentaries that have premiered at Sundance, Hot Docs, and CPH:DOX. She assembles silence as much as image — building films from the negative space between moments.

Her editorial philosophy resists the obvious: she is drawn to footage that resists easy interpretation, and she finds meaning in the pauses others would cut. Working closely with Marta Hryhorenko since 2019, she has become an inseparable part of the studio's creative voice.

Olena studied film editing at the Łódź Film School and currently teaches a masterclass in documentary editing at the Kyiv School of Economics cultural programme.

Who We Are

MOVIE is an independent documentary production studio founded on the belief that truth is the most radical form of storytelling. We work at the intersection of cinema, journalism, and art — crafting films that demand attention and provoke thought.

From long-form investigations to intimate short portraits, every project we undertake begins with a single question: what is the world trying not to see?
