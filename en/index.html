A documentary about the Ukrainian-American artist Yakiv Hnizdovsky, whose journey from DP camps to world recognition mirrors the experience of Ukrainian emigrants. Through the eyes of his cat Zherom, the film tells a story of the search for identity, a connection to nature, and overcoming loneliness in a changing world.

In Theaters 70 min Ukraine 2025 Documentary
Director Nadiya Parfan
Producers Natalia Movshovych, Nastya Kanarova, Marharyta Kulichova, Serhii Nedzelskyi, Alona Holiakova
  • 2025110th anniversary of Yakiv Hnizdovsky

Yakiv Hnizdovsky is one of the most significant Ukrainian artists of the 20th century, who created a recognizable universe of animals, plants, and refined forms through printmaking, painting, and design. His biography is a journey through DP camps, impoverished years in America, and late recognition - one that resonates with the experience of millions of displaced migrants. But in this film, the story is told not by the artist himself, but by the one who shared his years of solitude and creation - the cat Zherom.

A mockumentary hero who has lived through nine lives returns to Ukraine for Hnizdovsky's 110th anniversary to untangle the secrets of his legacy. Together with curators, researchers, scholars, and schoolchildren, he travels from Pylypche in the Ternopil region to New York, piecing together fragments of the artist's life into a single picture.

Weaving together archival material, interviews, animation, and cinematic scenes, the film reveals Hnizdovsky as a man who expressed his own philosophical search through a love of animals and nature. It is a story about slowness, contemplation, and the attempt to preserve one's self far from home - as relevant for Ukrainians today as it was a hundred years ago.

An intimate portrait of a couple whose love unfolds at a distance and under constant public attention.

In Theaters 21 min Ukraine 2026 Documentary · Short
Director Diana Anikieieva
Producer Nadiya Parfan
  • 2026Festivals TBA

When 21-year-old Pavlo, a soldier who openly speaks about his homosexuality, shares his story with the media, he becomes a symbol of visibility for the queer community in Ukraine. Together with his partner Vlad, he increasingly becomes part of public discourse.

Their relationship develops at a distance - through phone calls, rare visits, and moments lived under constant attention. Meetings that should be private often unfold under the gaze of cameras and interviews.

Tension gradually builds. Distance, jealousy, and unspoken trauma complicate their bond, while public expectations contrast with their everyday reality. Through fragments of communication and shared moments, "Tell Me a Fairy Tale" observes how intimacy is tested when a relationship exists both in private and in the public eye.

Nadiya Parfan
Director and Producer

Co-founder of the "86" festival and Takflix. Her debut film was named best documentary of the year in Ukraine.

Nadiya Parfan is a director and producer from Kyiv, co-founder of the "86" International Festival of Cinema and Urban Studies, the Takflix online cinema, and the 86PROKAT distribution company. Her debut feature documentary "Singing Power Plant" (2019) was named best documentary of the year by the Ukrainian Film Academy and the Union of Film Critics of Ukraine, and was later acquired by the Japanese broadcaster NHK.

Her short essay-film "I Didn't Want to Make a Film About the War" (2023) was released with the support of The New Yorker, and her fiction debut "This Is a Date" received a special jury mention at the Berlin International Film Festival. At Phalanstery Films, Nadiya combines directing with producing the studio's other projects.

Illia Hladshtein
Producer

Co-owner of the KINO42 cinema and the 86PROKAT distribution company. Produces the studio's documentary films, including "Where Everything Disappears."

Illia Hladshtein is a film producer, co-owner and managing partner of the Kyiv cinema KINO42, and owner of the distribution company 86PROKAT. In 2014-2018 he was co-founder and program director of the "86" International Festival of Cinema and Urban Studies, and as a producer released the film "Singing Power Plant" - the first Ukrainian documentary acquired by the Japanese broadcaster NHK.

At Phalanstery Films, Illia produces the studio's documentary films, including Oleksandr Tkachenko's "Where Everything Disappears," presented at the FIPADOC 2026 festival.

Diana Anikieieva
Director

Director of "Tell Me a Fairy Tale" - a film about the relationship of a military couple through a year of separation.

Diana Anikieieva is a documentary director, author of "Tell Me a Fairy Tale," which over the course of a year follows the relationship of two military partners through phone calls, meetings, and public appearances. The film received a special pitching jury mention at the Sunny Bunny festival and a special production mention from AMO Pictures at "Mykolaichuk OPEN" 2026.

In the director's words, she wanted to show that the relationships of military couples are no different from anyone else's, with their own challenges shaped by distance and war. The film's producer is Nadiya Parfan, produced by Phalanstery Films.

Oleksandr Tkachenko
Director

Director and anthropologist, author of "Where Everything Disappears," made with the support of ARTE.

Oleksandr Tkachenko is a director, journalist, and anthropologist who for nearly twenty years has created author-driven documentary content for national media, working with themes of social and cultural anthropology, minority life, and history. "Where Everything Disappears" is his first international feature film, made with the support of ARTE as part of the Generation Ukraine project; the film's world premiere took place at FIPADOC 2026.

Among the director's previous works are "Being Equal: Untold Stories," "Family Portrait," "Anthropology," and "Odesa, That Is Disappearing." After completing work on the film, Oleksandr joined the Armed Forces of Ukraine.

Who We Are

Phalanstery Films is an independent documentary production studio founded on the belief that truth is the most radical form of storytelling. We work at the intersection of cinema, journalism, and art.

From long-form investigations to intimate short portraits, every project we undertake begins with a single question: what is the world trying not to see?
