Synopsis

Before the first word, there was sound. Before sound, there was vibration.

Ocean Voice begins in the place where all vibration originates — the deep ocean — and follows the question of what it means to listen to something that speaks in frequencies the human body can only feel, not hear. Over twenty-eight minutes, the film moves between research vessels and coastlines, between scientists with hydrophones and fishermen who have navigated by the sea's moods for generations.

Neither group has the complete picture.
Both know more than they can say.

What Ocean Voice proposes — gently, without insisting — is that the sea is not a backdrop to human history. It is a participant. One with its own testimony, now increasingly muffled by the noise we have poured into it. To hear it again is not a romantic act. It is a necessary one.
