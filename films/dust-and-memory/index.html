Synopsis

Memory is not a record. It is a negotiation between what happened and what we can bear to carry. Over two years and five cities — Kyiv, Warsaw, Tbilisi, Minsk, and Lviv — Dust & Memory follows the invisible architecture of remembrance: the objects kept, the languages abandoned, the names given to children.

The film does not argue. It observes. It moves between a grandmother's apartment in Kyiv and a market stall in Tbilisi, between archival photographs and the faces of those who can still name the people in them. What emerges is a portrait of Eastern Europe not as a geopolitical category but as a geography of grief and continuity.
