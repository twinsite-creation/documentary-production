Synopsis

Lines on maps have always been lies. Iron Cartography traces the physical and psychological geography of a conflict zone — not through combat footage, but through the landscape that remains after: the blown bridge, the orchard no one harvests, the village whose name appears on maps from two different countries.

Working closely with cartographers, historians, and the displaced, the film asks how identity survives the redrawing of borders — and whether the old maps, stored in archives and in memory, constitute an act of resistance or simply a form of grief.
