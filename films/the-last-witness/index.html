Synopsis

They were children when it happened. Now, in their final decades, they carry the weight of memory no archive can replicate. The Last Witness follows three elderly Ukrainians — a retired schoolteacher, a former engineer, and a market vendor — each a living archive of events the world is still struggling to name.

Directed in a single, uninterrupted observational mode, the film asks what it means to remember alone. As their generation disappears, so does a particular texture of truth — intimate, unverifiable, irreplaceable.
