Synopsis

A city does not change all at once. It transforms through the accumulation of a thousand quiet decisions — a window boarded, a language whispered instead of spoken, a name erased from a building's façade. Shadows of Kyiv follows five residents across three years of radical upheaval, tracing how the public and the private collapse into each other when history refuses to stay outside.

Filmed in intimate proximity, the documentary resists the spectacular in favour of the everyday. What emerges is not a chronicle of events, but a portrait of endurance — of a city that refuses to become its own ruin.
