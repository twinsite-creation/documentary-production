Synopsis

In the vaults of the Dovzhenko Film Centre in Kyiv lies footage that was never shown, stories that were completed but not permitted. Silent Archive excavates the Ukrainian poetic cinema of the 1960s — the so-called Shistdesiatnyky, or Sixtiers — whose films were shelved by Soviet censors and whose names were erased from official histories.

Through interviews with archivists, surviving filmmakers, and their descendants, the film reconstructs not only the works themselves but the aesthetic philosophy they embodied: a cinema of light, landscape, and quiet insurgency. What emerges is a portrait of an art form that refused to disappear.
